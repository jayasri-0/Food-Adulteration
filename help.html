Frequently Asked Questions

What are the common adulterants in everyday foods?

Common adulterants include chalk powder in flour, starch in milk, artificial color in spices, and water in milk. Each food item has specific adulterants that can impact its quality and safety.

What tests are available to check for adulterants at home?

Some simple tests include adding iodine to milk to check for starch, dropping a bit of turmeric powder in water to check for artificial color, and using a magnet over rice grains to detect iron filings. These tests vary depending on the food item.
